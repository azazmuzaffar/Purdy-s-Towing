ERICA BOOTHB.A., Behavioral Health Aide 3

Erica graduated from the University of Minnesota-Morris in 2019 with a bachelor’s in liberal arts, majoring in Psychology. Erica was a member of the Equity, Diversity, and Intercultural Program in Morris. She also worked under Heather Peters, Ph.D, as a research assistant conducting research in the development of culturally sensitive interventions and programs to improve the health and wellbeing of people from marginalized cultural groups. She began working at Wil la mootk Counseling Center in the summer of 2016. Erica continued to work over the summers while earning her bachelor’s degree from 2017-2019. During that time, Erica worked in administration, trained as a Behavioral Health Aide (BHA), worked with clients, and had shifts at Wap Gyilks Amaniisgn.

After graduating in 2019, Erica started working full time at Wil la mootk as a BHA. In 2020, she received her certification for BHA III. Erica loves working with the youth and families within Metlakatla. She is passionate about teaching and practicing healthy living skills. She enjoys fitness, hiking with her two dogs, and spending time with her husband.

MEGAN HANIFINL.P.C., Individual and Family Therapist

Megan joined the Wil la mootk team in 2022. Originally from Hawaii, Megan graduated from the University of Washington in 2003 with a bachelor’s degree in literature. She moved to Alaska in 2006 and pursued a variety of jobs, including commercial fishing. In 2008, she joined a peer support group, and experienced firsthand the power of psychotherapy to heal and transform lives. She was inspired to pursue a career in mental health counseling, and completed a Master of Science in Counseling Psychology at Capella University in 2012. Before moving to Metlakatla, Megan worked as a Licensed Professional Counselor at several agencies in Ketchikan, gaining knowledge and experience especially in working with children in the foster care system and with survivors of domestic violence and other traumas.

Megan is passionate about helping people to see their own innocence and to feel hopeful about their futures. Megan’s greatest joy in life is being a mother to her 10-year-old son. She is grateful to be able to raise him in Metlakatla where he can experience a close-knit community and learn about indigenous culture and values.

Allison RobbinsL.P.C. Individual and Family Therapist

Allison joined the Wil la mootk Counseling Center as a clinician in January of this year 2023 and is our newest addition to the team. Passionate about the immaculate nature and mysteries of Psyche, she enjoys curating creative and explorative space for individuals and groups. Her avid curiosities have led her to the jungles of Costa Rica and Columbia where she studied alternative healing and the repressed feminine mythologies. These discoveries catapulted her back to her homeland of California where she worked as a dancer, filmmaker, and ceremonialist. Trauma, a taboo subject, is one of her muses as she sees contending with it as an initiatory gateway into a greater Eros-infused life.

Some of her previous work has focused on bringing the arts to foster youth and individuals wrestling with homelessness, addiction, victims and survivors of sexual exploitation, and souls on the precipice of major breakthroughs.

Along with her Masters of Arts in Clinical and Depth Psychology, she is also trained and certified in Danyasa Yoga Arts, Shadow Work facilitation, Shamanic Fusion Dance, is a graduate of multiple mystery schools, and is an ordained priestess in the medicine of movement. She is currently working towards her Ph.D. in Archetypal Studies.

In her free time Allison enjoys international flights, writing, dancing her prayers into the ocean, making experimental films, hiking, and dinners out with friends and family. While she loves California, her heart is enjoying opening to the idiosyncrasies and beauties of Alaska. The Pacific Ocean is one she is loyal to.

NATHAN FAWCETTAdministrative Floater

Nathan has worked at Wil la mootk Counseling Center since February 2022. He is currently floating on a sea of administrative duties (Administrative Floater) for the Wil La Mootk Counseling Center, which includes coordinating with the Executive Director, Business Manager and Wil La Mootk staff with day-to-day operations, policy, quality assurance, and billing.

Nathan has held many positions within the Metlakatla Indian Community over the years including Executive Treasurer for 5 terms, Roads Coordinator, Geographic Information Systems Technician for MIC Forestry, Information Technology Technician for MIC, and Grant Coordinator for Annette Island Packing Co. Training Grant, Technology Upgrade, Tunnel Freezer, and Piling Replacement projects.

Nathan earned an Associate of Fine Arts in 3-D design, focused on metalsmithing and jewelry fabrication, from the Institute of American Arts after transferring from the University of Alaska Fairbanks. He also has an Associate of Science in Business Administration and Associate of Science in Computer Science from the Southwestern Indian Polytechnic Institute, where he was also a member of the American Indian Science & Engineering Society and Phi Theta Kappa honors society.

Nathan is an avid reader, gamer, concert go-er, traveler, and enjoys bingeing shows with his benevolent kitty overlord, Zubes. Nathan has also volunteered on the Board of Directors and Metlakatla Advisory Committee for the Tongass Federal Credit Union. He is proud to be part of the team at Wil La Mootk providing sustainable services which contribute to the positive mental health and wellbeing of the community of Metlakatla.

TIFFINEY L. McKEEHANL.C.S.W., Executive Director

Tiffiney graduated from the University of Alaska Southeast in 2012 with a bachelor’s in liberal arts with a concentration in Psychology and Sub-Concentration in Sociology. While completing her master’s degree she worked as a student ambassador and interned with the Housing Authority piloting a social work program for elders. She graduated in 2015 from the University of Southern California with a Master of Social Work and a Concentration in Mental Health and Sub Concentration in Older Adults; and became a Licensed Clinical Social Worker in 2020. She went on to earn a graduate certificate in Leadership from Walden University in 2021.

Tiffiney has been with Wil la mootk since 2016. She has worked in every clinical role including Therapeutic Foster Parent, Behavioral Health Aid, Behavioral Health Aid Supervisor, Clinician, Lead Clinician, Acting Director, Interim Director, and finally, Executive Director in 2021. This experience gives her a unique perspective of how the organization functions and how the team can work more cohesively.

Tiffiney has been with her husband for 23 years and together are raising their three sons. Metlakatla is her hometown and the people of the community are her motivation for school, training, and all work activities to increase their quality of life. As a lifelong learner and community member Tiffiney finds her life’s work incredibly fulfilling!

COREY GOHEENBA, Foster Care Case Manager

Corey has worked at Wil la mootk Counseling Center since September 2022 as Foster Care Case Manager. She also holds the Chair position for the Family Focused Treatment Association for the Alaska Chapter and has held an officer position since 2014 in this association. Corey also is the head of the parent teachers committee for Criag Headstart. Corey is originally from the NY and NJ area, however, spent twelve years living, going to school and then working and volunteering in adult mental health agencies in Tucson. She followed her husband home from Tucson to Prince of Wales, Alaska in 2012 where they later had two children together residing in Craig.

Corey had worked the previous ten years as Foster Care Manager of Community Connections prior to transferring to Wil la mootk and also leading community initiatives to spread awareness of resilience under the Prince of Wales Health Network goals by developing summit and trainings available to communities. Corey received her BA in Psychology with a minor in Family Studies Human Development from University of Arizona and currently is working toward her MSW at Walden University. She has also been an officially certified trainer for Pressley Ridge for the past ten years, training the evidenced based curriculum to ensure that best quality care is delivered throughout therapeutic foster families. She enjoys cooking, spending time with her family, and helping others in her free time. Coreys main passion is to help children, youth and families and contribute to the overall wellbeing of the communities she serves.

SEAN TOMKINSONL.C.S.W., Family & Individual Therapist

Sean joined team Wil la mookt in December of 2022. He’s a father of 4 and a grandfather of 12. Sean came to Prince of Wales Island in Alaska 14 years ago from Idaho where he grew up on a cattle and wheat farm. Small towns have always been his thing and he graduated with 8 other students in his high school senior class. He took a roundabout way getting into the behavioral health world. His experience includes 10 years in the US Air Force, working in avionics and air traffic control.

After the Air Force, he completed his bachelor of social work degree at Idaho State University, while working for the US Postal Service. He earned his Master of Social Work from Walla Wall University in 2002. He has worked in both direct care and in various supervisory capacities. His experience includes emergency room crisis management, inpatient psychiatric hospital, community mental health, and wilderness therapy. He has a passion for the outdoors and loves using nature as a therapeutic tool. He comments that “healing happens as we mindfully engage in healthy relationships” and that “Nature is the greatest healer of all.”

Jezimay Calf Boss RibsBehavioral Health Aide 1

Shallyn started working with the Wil la mootk counseling center in January 2021 and during this time, she has worked diligently towards her Behavioral Health Aide certification. Shallyn began demonstrating an interest in mental health early in life. In high school, she and a friend started a suicide prevention initiative. At a young age she harnessed the power of social media and her creativity to connect with those may be struggling with suicidal thoughts.

She is excited to learn and grow professionally and is always thinking of new ways to make mental health fun and relatable to children and adolescents. Shallyn has volunteered with students throughout her middles school and high school career. Shallyn enjoys practicing music and dancing to express herself and hopes to teach the children of the community other healthy, creative, and self-expressive habits to better cope with daily life.

Shallyn has pieces of her heart between two locations and travels from Metlakatla to Puyallup to see her loved ones. She enjoys spending time with her partner and cat and when the weather permits, practicing mindfulness near the water at Point D. Shallyn has always had a passion for helping people. She continues to provide excellent services to our clients with care and dedication to a client-centered, strength-based approach.

SYLVIA BLANDOVBehavioral Health Aide 2 / CDC-1 / Behavioral Health Aide Lead

Sylvia’s passion has always been for children and youth. Her hope is that every individual finds their voice and feels valued and loved.

Sylvia’s primary background is in Early Childhood Education. She loved her 22 year career as a Head Start teacher. Ready for a career change, she wanted to help others. She became an advocate at WISH and supported women and children affected by domestic violence/sexual assault.

Over the years, Sylvia assisted in establishing programs for children and youth in the following organizations: Tlingit and Haida Head Start, Women In Safe Homes, Community Connections Early Learning Program and MIC Early Head Start. Sylvia has also served on the Saxman IRA Council, KIC Native Education Board, T & H Parent Board, Fawn Mountain Education Board, and the WISH Board.

Sylvia continues to love being a Wellness Counselor and has since May 2014. Currently, she is the BHA Lead and holds a Behavioral Health Aide II certification and a Chemical Dependency Counselor I certification. Sylvia considers it an honor to be invited to walk next to our community’s children and youth to offer love and support as each continues on their journey to overall health and wellness.

EDI GUTHRIEAdministrative Assistant

Edith Guthrie has been a hobby sized business owner selling pizza and breakfast bagel sandwiches. During the early part of that business, she noticed Wil la mootk Counseling Center – its great public work in the community and how its presence made “A Place of healing” inviting and comforting. At first, she was asked to cater for the grand opening of the new building, and this began her wish to be more involved.

Edi began her work at Wil la mootk in May 2017 as Administrative Assistant. Happy to become a part of healing in her own hometown of Metlakatla, Alaska, she enjoyed helping every part of Team Wil la mootk “Make It Happen”. In 2019 she became Business Manager and delved deeper into learning policies and procedures for Wil la mootk Counseling Center, CARF, MIC, State of Alaska and Federal regulations.

Edi has a background that includes a certificate in Medical Billing and Office Automation but also a vast collection of job types, her favorites being an Optician and work as a cook & deckhand on the AMHS ferry system. Being a wife and mother is the first part of her life’s work, yet is currently she is working at finishing her bachelor’s degree in business management with a Human Resource Certificate and is also studying to become certified in billing and compliance. Edi’s greatest endeavor is to create awareness and increase opportunities for people to heal in every part of their lives and experience.
