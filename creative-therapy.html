Creative

Therapy

Creative therapy, also known as expressive therapy or creative arts therapy, is an approach to psychotherapy that utilizes various forms of creative expression to support individuals in exploring and addressing emotional, psychological, and behavioral concerns.

ENROLL NOW

Creative Therapy recognizes the power of creative processes and artistic mediums in promoting self-expression, personal growth, and healing. Creative therapy can incorporate different art forms, such as visual arts (painting or drawing), music, dance, writing, traditional or cultural art forms, and more. The specific medium used will depend on the client’s preferences, strengths, and therapeutic goals.

BENEFITS OF
CREATIVE
THERAPY

In Creative Therapy, a therapist may guide individuals through various creative forms or mediums to find the Best method that suits that individual and is in alignment with their particular goals.

Creative Therapy can be highly effective in treating a range of mental health conditions, such as:

  • Depression & Anxiety Disorders
  • Trauma and Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Substance Abuse or Addiction
  • Eating Disorders
  • Grief or Loss
  • Stress & Burnout

Creative Therapy can also be useful for individuals Who are seeking personal growth, self-exploration, and increased self-awareness.

  • Explore fears, worries or underlying emotions
  • Provide a calming feeling that promotes relaxation
  • Reduction of stress
  • Increased self-confidence and self-esteem
  • Safe outlet for healing and recovery from trauma
  • Foster a healthy outlet for self-expression and draw connections to culture and our way of life.

HOW DOES
IT WORK?

Creative therapy is conducted by trained and licensed professionals, who have expertise in both the creative process and psychotherapy techniques, allowing them to integrate art-based interventions into the therapeutic process effectively.

Some examples may include:

  • Beading
  • Painting or drawing
  • Carving or Sculpting
  • Regalia making
  • Music, Singing or Songwriting
