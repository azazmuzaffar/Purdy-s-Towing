Therapy

Interpersonal Neurobiology (IPNB) therapy is an approach to psychotherapy that integrates principles from neuroscience, attachment theory, and interpersonal relationships.

ENROLL NOW

IPNB Therapy, also considered One-On-One Counseling/Therapy, emphasizes the interconnectedness between the brain, the mind (including thoughts, emotions, and beliefs), and relationships.

BENEFITS OF
INTERPERSONAL
NEUROBIOLOGY
THERAPY

In IPNB Therapy, the therapist aims to create a safe and attuned therapeutic relationship that supports the client’s exploration of their thoughts, emotions, and relational patterns. The therapist helps the client develop awareness of their neural and bodily sensations, thoughts, and emotions, and supports the integration of these experiences for healing and growth.

IPNB Therapy can be highly effective in treating a range of mental health conditions, such as:

  • Depression & Anxiety Disorders
  • Trauma and Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Relationship difficulties
  • Emotional dysregulation
  • Personal growth and improved well-being
  • Overall self-awareness

One-on-One Therapy can also offer other several benefits for individuals such as:

  • Improved self-awareness
  • Enhanced emotional regulation
  • Improved relationship skills
  • Increased resilience
  • Trauma healing
  • Positive brain changes and mindfulness

HOW DOES
IT WORK?

The expertise and guidance of a qualified IPNB therapist are essential in tailoring the approach to meet each person’s unique circumstances.

Your licensed and trained professional may use a variety of techniques such as integration, mindfulness, attachment theory, co-regulation, and more.

You may expect:

  • A collaborative therapeutic relationship
  • Exploration of thoughts, emotions, and experiences
  • Integration of body and mind
  • Practice of self-compassion
  • Personalized exercises
