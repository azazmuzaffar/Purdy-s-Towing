Play

Therapy

Play therapy utilizes play and imagination, A child’s natural form of expression, to help them express their feelings more easily through toys instead of words.

ENROLL NOW

Not only can play therapy provide insight through observation, but a trained and licensed
professional can help the child explore emotions that children typically are not
able to articulate on their own.

BENEFITS OF
PLAY THERAPY

Many different types of people can benefit from play therapy, though typically used for children between ages of 3-12.

Play Therapy may be helpful when undergoing these type of circumstances and beyond:

  • Behavioral problems at school or home
  • Traumatic events
  • Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Loss of a family member or loved one
  • Developmental delay or learning disabilities

Play therapy can encourage the use of language, fine and gross motor skills, but the potential benefits don’t stop there:

  • Self-respect
  • Empathy and respect for others
  • Alleviation of anxiety
  • Learning to express feelings in a healthy way
  • Develop stronger social skills
  • Strengthen family bonds or relationships

HOW DOES
IT WORK?

When it comes to adults and children, there is a bit of a communication gap. A child may feel something, but not understand the method or means of how to express it, or they don’t have an adult or trusted companion they can express it to. There are also instances where adults can misinterpret a childs behavior, verbal, or non-verbal cues. Depending on the child’s stage of development and age, they may not have the language skills they need to address their emotions.

Children learn to understand and express complex emotions and theories through play. This is where they are free to act out with their toys as characters that take on a greater meaning. They can act out fears or anxieties, or even problem-solve. Each child is different and has unique needs. Wil La Mootk conducts a thorough assessment with a trained and licensed professional to tailor individualized therapeutic goals, limits, or plans for each child.

All sessions take place in a neutral environment where the child can feel safe, In our Canoe Room & —— Room, a variety of toys are available to spark their Play therapy. Sessions typically last — to — minutes.

Some examples may include:

  • Creative Storytelling
  • Musical Play
  • Imaginative Play
  • Games
  • Sensory Related Toys such as Sand or Water
  • Dolls or Action Figures
  • Building Blocks
